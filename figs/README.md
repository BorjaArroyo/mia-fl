# Theory Simulation Figures

This directory contains utility plots generated by `plot_results.py` to verify the theoretical bounds and simulate the TS-MIA risk.

## Figures

### 1. `verify_sur_geometry.png` (Stage I)
**Title:** Stage I: Signal Survival Geometry
**Description:** Visualizes the Signal-to-Update Ratio (SUR) as a function of the alignment $\lambda$ (x-axis) and the magnitude ratio $r = ||B||/||u||$ (y-axis).
-   **Dark Purple/Blue Regions:** High SUR (Signal Dominance).
-   **Yellow/Green Regions:** Low SUR (Signal Cancellation/Masking).
-   **Red Overlay:** Cancellation region where $||\Delta W|| < \epsilon$.
-   **Plotting Note:** Range is restricted to $r \le 6$ with a log color scale for readability.
-   **Key Insight:** High SUR occurs when the background is small ($r \to 0$), even for $\lambda \approx -1$ (signal survives). True cancellation happens when $\lambda \approx -1$ and $r \approx 1$, where $\Delta W \approx 0$ and detectability becomes noise-limited.

### 2. `verify_attribution_bound.png` (Stage II)
**Title:** Stage II: Smoothness Bound Verification
**Description:** Verifies the quadratic remainder bound on the score increment $\delta(x;u)$.
-   **X-axis:** Update norm $||u||$.
-   **Y-axis:** Approximation error $|\delta(x;u) - \langle \nabla \ell, u \rangle|$.
-   **Red Dashed Line:** The theoretical quadratic bound $\frac{L}{2}||u||^2$, estimated from a calibration split.
-   **Note:** The violation rate is computed using a per-trial $L_{\mathrm{cal}}$; the red line is a representative curve, so some points may lie above it.
-   **Key Insight:** Calibration and test points are separated; test errors falling below the bound confirm the smoothness assumption used in Stage II.

### 3. `stage_two_geometry.png` (Stage II)
**Title:** Stage II Geometry: Attribution vs. Alignment
**Description:** Attribution score $J(u)$ vs alignment $\cos \theta$ for a small, representative set of update magnitudes.
-   **Panels:** 3–4 representative magnitudes $||u||$.
-   **Solid Line:** Actual attribution score.
-   **Dotted Line:** Linear proxy (gradient alignment).
-   **Key Insight:** Small magnitudes follow the linear proxy closely; larger magnitudes show controlled deviation.

### 4. `risk_trajectory.png` (Risk Simulation)
**Title:** Risk Evolution by Data Scenario
**Description:** Tracks the evolution of the TS-MIA risk metric over Federated Learning rounds.
-   **X-axis:** FL Round.
-   **Y-axis:** Detection Rate (Fraction of clients with SUR > Threshold AND positive Attribution).
-   **Curves:**
    -   **IID (Blue):** Baseline risk.
    -   **Non-IID (Orange):** Risk under label skew (usually lower due to orthogonality).
    -   **Inverted (Red):** Risk under adversarial/cancellation conditions (lowest risk).

### 9. `non_iid_image_risk.png` (Risk Simulation)
**Title:** Non-IID Example + Metrics
**Description:** Shows a representative Non-IID sample image (from a label-skewed client) alongside round-wise Non-IID metrics.
-   **Left Panel:** Example PathMNIST image from the Non-IID partition (client index and label shown).
-   **Right Panel:** Mean Risk, Mean SUR, and Mean J across rounds (with CI if multiple seeds), sharing the same y-axis bounds.
